**Mid-level IT jobs** are positions that typically require 3–7+ years of relevant experience, bridging entry-level support roles and senior/lead or managerial positions. These roles involve greater independence, complex problem-solving, specialization, and often some mentoring or project leadership, without full senior-level strategic or people-management responsibilities.

### Common Mid-Level IT Roles
Here are some of the most typical mid-level positions across IT domains:

- **Systems Administrator (SysAdmin)**: Manages, configures, and maintains servers, operating systems (Windows/Linux), virtualization (e.g., VMware), and user access. Troubleshoots issues and ensures uptime and security.
- **Network Administrator / Engineer**: Designs, implements, and maintains LAN/WAN networks, routers, switches, firewalls, and VPNs. Handles performance monitoring and basic troubleshooting.
- **IT Analyst / Business Systems Analyst**: Analyzes business needs, translates them into technical requirements, and supports system implementations or improvements.
- **Database Administrator (DBA)**: Manages databases (SQL/NoSQL), optimizes queries, ensures data integrity, backups, and security.
- **Cybersecurity Analyst / Specialist**: Monitors for threats, implements security controls, conducts vulnerability assessments, responds to incidents, and ensures compliance.
- **Software Engineer / Developer (Mid-Level)**: Designs, codes, tests, and maintains applications. Works more independently on features, code reviews, and integrations (often full-stack, backend, or frontend focus).
- **Cloud Engineer / Administrator**: Manages cloud infrastructure (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud), deployments, scaling, cost optimization, and hybrid environments.
- **IT Project Coordinator / Junior Project Manager**: Oversees smaller projects, coordinates resources, tracks timelines/budgets, and uses tools like Jira or Microsoft Project.
- **DevOps Engineer**: Bridges development and operations with automation, CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code (e.g., Terraform), and monitoring tools.
- **Quality Assurance (QA) Engineer / Tester**: Develops and executes test plans, automation scripts, and ensures software quality (mid-level often includes automation frameworks).

Other possibilities include roles like Security Operations Center (SOC) Analyst, Data Analyst (mid-career), or specialized support in areas like telecommunications or enterprise applications.

### Typical Requirements
- **Experience**: 3–7 years in IT, often progressing from help desk, junior admin, or support roles.
- **Education**: Bachelor’s degree in IT, Computer Science, or a related field is common (though not always mandatory with strong experience and certifications).
- **Certifications** (highly valued): CompTIA (Network+, Security+), CCNA (Cisco), AWS/Azure certifications, CISSP (for security), PMP (for project roles), or vendor-specific ones (e.g., Microsoft, VMware).
- **Skills**: Deeper technical expertise in your domain, troubleshooting complex issues, scripting/automation (Python, PowerShell, Bash), documentation, collaboration, and basic project management or mentoring.

### Salary Ranges (US, Approximate as of ~2026)
Salaries vary significantly by location (higher in tech hubs like San Francisco, New York), industry, company size, and specific skills (e.g., cloud or cybersecurity command premiums). Overall IT median pay is well above the national average.

- **General range for mid-level roles**: $80,000–$130,000+ total compensation.
- **Examples**:
  - Systems/Network Admin: ~$85,000–$110,000 (BLS median for network admins around $97k).
  - Cybersecurity Analyst: ~$95,000–$120,000+.
  - Mid-level Software Engineer: Often $120,000–$150,000+.
  - IT Project Manager (mid): ~$100,000–$130,000.
  - Data Analyst (mid-career): ~$90,000–$110,000.

These figures can include bonuses, stock, or benefits.

### Career Progression Tips
Mid-level is a great stage to specialize (e.g., cloud, security, data) or broaden skills for senior roles. Gain hands-on experience with modern tech stacks, contribute to projects, pursue certifications, and build soft skills like communication and leadership. Many move into senior engineer, architect, team lead, or IT manager roles from here.

Demand remains strong due to digital transformation, cybersecurity threats, cloud adoption, and AI integration. Check sites like Indeed, LinkedIn, or Dice for current openings, and tailor your resume to highlight quantifiable achievements (e.g., "Reduced downtime by 40%" or "Managed migrations for 500+ users").
