**Entry-level IT jobs** are roles that typically require little to no prior professional experience. They often need a high school diploma, associate degree, relevant certifications (like CompTIA A+), or demonstrable skills through projects/bootcamps.

These positions serve as gateways into the field, focusing on foundational support, troubleshooting, and basic technical tasks. In 2026, the market is competitive—especially for pure beginners—due to AI automation of some routine tasks and selective hiring, but demand remains steady for support and foundational roles.

### Common Entry-Level IT Jobs
Here are some of the most accessible and in-demand options:

1. **IT Support Specialist / Help Desk Technician (Tier 1)**  
   - **What you do**: Troubleshoot hardware/software issues, reset passwords, assist users via phone/email/chat, basic network support.  
   - **Typical salary (US)**: $45,000–$65,000 (median around $50k–$64k).  
   - **Requirements**: CompTIA A+ often preferred or required; strong customer service and problem-solving skills.  
   - **Why it's great for starters**: Most common entry point; high volume of openings.

2. **Desktop Support Specialist**  
   - **What you do**: Install/configure computers, peripherals, and software; on-site or remote user support.  
   - **Typical salary**: $55,000–$74,000.  
   - **Requirements**: A+ certification, basic hardware knowledge.

3. **Technical Support Specialist (Product/Application Support)**  
   - **What you do**: Help customers with specific software or company products.  
   - **Typical salary**: Around $60k–$72k.  
   - Good for those with communication strengths.

4. **Junior Network Technician / Administrator**  
   - **What you do**: Assist with network setup, monitoring, and basic troubleshooting.  
   - **Typical salary**: $60k–$79k.  
   - **Requirements**: CompTIA Network+ is a big plus.

5. **Junior Cybersecurity / SOC Analyst** (more competitive)  
   - **What you do**: Monitor for threats, basic security tasks.  
   - **Typical salary**: Higher starting pay (often $60k+).  
   - **Requirements**: Security+ certification helps stand out.

6. **Other Notable Options**:
   - Data Center Technician
   - Field Service Technician
   - QA (Quality Assurance) Tester / Analyst
   - Junior Web Developer or Data Analyst (these lean more technical/coding)

### Key Certifications for Entry-Level Roles
- **CompTIA A+** — The gold standard for beginners (hardware, software, troubleshooting).
- **CompTIA Network+** — For networking roles.
- **CompTIA Security+** — Opens cybersecurity doors.
- Others: Google IT Support Professional Certificate, Microsoft certifications, or vendor-specific (e.g., AWS Cloud Practitioner).

Many roles value these over (or in addition to) a degree.

### 2026 Job Market Realities
- **Opportunities**: Strong in support roles; healthcare, government, MSPs (managed service providers), and smaller businesses often hire entry-level talent.
- **Challenges**: More competition; some "entry-level" postings now prefer 1–2 years experience or strong portfolios. AI handles some basic tasks, so emphasize human skills (communication, adaptability) and AI literacy.
- **Growth Path**: Many start in help desk and move to sysadmin, networking, cloud, cybersecurity, or engineering within 1–3 years.

**Tips to Land One**:
- Build a home lab or personal projects (e.g., set up a small network).
- Get certified (A+ is a high-ROI starting point).
- Apply broadly on Indeed, LinkedIn, Dice; consider contract/temp roles for experience.
- Highlight customer service or any tech troubleshooting from past jobs (even non-IT).

The IT field rewards continuous learning. Entry-level roles provide hands-on experience that pays off quickly with promotions and specialization.
