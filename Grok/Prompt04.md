**Entry-level IT certifications** validate foundational skills in hardware, software, troubleshooting, networking, and basic security. They help candidates without much experience stand out for roles like IT Support Specialist, Help Desk Technician, Desktop Support, and Junior Network Technician.

In 2026, employers still value these certs, especially vendor-neutral ones, though hands-on experience, projects, and soft skills (customer service, problem-solving) are equally important. The market is competitive, so pairing a cert with a strong resume and applications boosts your chances.

### Top Entry-Level IT Certifications (2026)

| Rank | Certification | Best For | Key Topics | Cost (approx.) | Time to Complete | Why It’s Valuable |
|------|---------------|----------|------------|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **CompTIA A+** | Absolute beginners, Help Desk, Desktop Support | Hardware, OS (Windows/macOS/Linux), troubleshooting, basic networking & security, virtualization | $250–$500 (two exams) | 1–3 months | Gold standard for entry-level IT; widely recognized by employers for support roles. |
| 2 | **Google IT Support Professional Certificate** | Career changers, complete novices | Troubleshooting, networking, OS, system admin, security, customer service | ~$49/month (Coursera) | 3–6 months (flexible) | Practical, job-ready focus; affordable and respected; good stepping stone to A+. |
| 3 | **CompTIA Network+** | Networking-focused roles | Networks, protocols, infrastructure, troubleshooting, security | ~$350 | 2–4 months (after A+) | Opens junior network tech/admin roles; highly recommended after A+. |
| 4 | **CompTIA Security+** | Cybersecurity interest | Threats, risk management, architecture, operations | ~$370 | 2–4 months | Entry into security roles (SOC Analyst); DoD-approved; strong demand. |
| 5 | **CompTIA Tech+ (ITF+)** | True beginners with zero background | Basic IT concepts, infrastructure, apps, security, data | Lower cost | 1–2 months | Gentle intro before A+; builds confidence. |

**Other strong options**:
- **Microsoft Azure Fundamentals (AZ-900)** or **AWS Cloud Practitioner** — Great if targeting cloud support roles.
- **Cisco CCNA** — More networking depth; ambitious for true beginners but powerful.
- **ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)** — Emerging entry-level cyber option.

### Recommended Path for Most Beginners
1. **Start here**: Google IT Support Certificate (affordable, practical) **or** CompTIA A+ (more recognized credential).
2. Add **Network+** for broader opportunities.
3. Specialize later (Security+, Cloud, etc.) after landing your first job.

Many people get their first IT job with just **A+** or the **Google certificate** plus applications and a home lab/portfolio.

### Key Tips for 2026
- **A+ is still worth it** for most true beginners, though some debate its depth—pair it with practical experience.
- Certifications alone don’t guarantee jobs — apply aggressively, build a simple home lab (e.g., virtual machines, basic networking), and highlight any customer service or troubleshooting from past roles.
- Renewals: Most CompTIA certs require continuing education (CEUs) every 3 years.
- Free/cheap resources: Professor Messer (A+), Coursera, YouTube, official practice exams.
