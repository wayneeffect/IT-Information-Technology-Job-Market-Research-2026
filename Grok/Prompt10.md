**The global IT (Information Technology) market is massive and growing steadily**, but its exact size depends on the definition used (e.g., total IT spending vs. IT services vs. broader technology market). Here are the most credible 2026 figures as of mid-2026:

### 1. **Worldwide IT Spending (Most Common Benchmark)**
   - **2026 Projection**: **$6.0 – $6.3 trillion** (Gartner forecasts range from ~$6.08T to $6.31T).
   - This includes hardware, software, IT services, communications, and data center systems.
   - Growth: Expected **9.8% to 13.5%** increase from 2025, driven heavily by **AI infrastructure**, cloud, and data centers.

### 2. **IT Services Market** (Outsourcing, consulting, managed services, etc.)
   - **2025/2026**: Roughly **$1.5 – $1.7 trillion**.
   - **Projections**: Expected to reach **$2.5 – $3.3 trillion** by 2033–2035, with CAGRs of **7–9%**.

### 3. **Broader Information Technology Market** (Varies widely by source)
   - Some reports value the overall IT market at **~$9–14 trillion** in 2026, growing toward **$13–36 trillion** by 2030–2035 (CAGR ~7–11%). These broader figures often include hardware manufacturing, telecom, software, and more.

### Key Growth Drivers in 2026
- **Artificial Intelligence** (especially infrastructure and generative AI tools).
- Cloud computing and data center expansion.
- Cybersecurity needs.
- Digital transformation across industries (healthcare, finance, manufacturing, government).

**North America** (especially the U.S.) dominates, often accounting for ~40%+ of the market, followed by Europe and Asia-Pacific.

**Summary**:
- **Current (2026)**: ~**$6 trillion** in total global IT spending (the figure most analysts and executives reference).
- **Near-term outlook**: Strong mid-to-high single-digit growth annually through the early 2030s, fueled by AI and cloud.
